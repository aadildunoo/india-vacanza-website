Tour Package 1: Golden Triangle

5 Nights / 6 Days covering Delhi, Agra, and Jaipur.

An ideal trip for a first time visitor to India. The renowned and popular Golden Triangle of Delhi, Agra and Jaipur, epitomizes in a way no other area can match, a lyrical vision of romantic India highlighting some of the great delights and most memorable sights of Mughal and Rajput India.

Day 01 – Arrive Delhi

You will be met at the airport by India Vacanza representative and transferred to the hotel and assisted with check in formalities. Delhi is the capital of India, and its third largest city. Delhi consists of two parts – New Delhi, the city created in 1911 as the imperial capital of India by the British and the Old Delhi, the Muslim capital of India between the 17th and 19th centuries.

Day 02 – In Delhi

Guided sightseeing tour of Old & New Delhi. Visits include:

  • Red Fort: A massive red sandstone fort built by Shah Jahan.
  • Jama Masjid: Country’s largest mosque.
  • Chandni Chowk: Colorful main street and shopping bazaar.
  • Raj Ghat: Mahatma Gandhi’s memorial.
  • India Gate: War memorial arch.
  • Humayun’s Tomb & Qutub Minar: Iconic historical landmarks.
Day 03 – Drive Delhi - Agra (220 kms/04 hrs)

Drive to Agra, home to the Taj Mahal. Afternoon tour of:

Taj Mahal: Built by Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his wife Mumtaj Mahal. It took 22 years and 20,000 craftsmen to complete.

Agra Fort: A massive red sandstone fort predominantly built by Emperor Akbar.

Day 04 – Drive Agra - Jaipur (250 kms/05 hrs)

Drive to Jaipur, stopping enroute at Fatehpur Sikri, the deserted capital of Emperor Akbar. Arrive in Jaipur, the "Pink City", founded in 1727 by Maharaja Jai Singh II.

Day 05 – In Jaipur

Guided tour of Jaipur and Amber:

  • Hawa Mahal: The Palace of Winds with its pink windows.
  • City Palace: Home of former royalty with extensive art collections.
  • Jantar Mantar: 18th-century astronomical observatory.
  • Amber Fort: Superb example of Rajput architecture overlooking a lake.
Day 06 – Drive Jaipur - Delhi & Depart

Drive back to Delhi's International Airport in time for your flight home.

Tour Package 2: Glimpses of the South

11 Nights / 12 Days covering Kerala and Tamil Nadu.

A 12 day holiday taking you to some of the most beautiful and culturally rich destinations in Southern India. The trip covers the most important sights of Kerala and Tamil Nadu.

Day 01 - Arrive Cochin

Met at the airport by India Vacanza representative and transferred to the hotel. Cochin, the "Queen of the Arabian Sea," boasts a rich cultural heritage and is one of the finest natural harbors in the world.

Day 02 – Cochin

Guided sightseeing tour includes:

  • Dutch Palace: 16th-century palace with astonishing Hindu murals.
  • Jewish Synagogue: Magnificently decorated with Chinese tiles and Belgian chandeliers.
  • Cochin Harbor: Famous for its unique Chinese fishing nets.
  • St Francis Church: Oldest European-built church in India, once the burial site of Vasco da Gama.

Evening: Traditional Kathakali dance performance at a local theatre.

Day 03 – Drive Cochin – Kumarakom (85 kms/02 hrs)

Drive to Kumarakom, a cluster of little islands on the Vembanad Lake. Known for mangrove forests, coconut groves, and emerald green paddy fields. Enjoy the resort’s facilities.

Day 04 – Drive Kumarakom – Periyar (200 kms / 4 hrs)

Drive to Periyar (Thekkady), one of the best places to see the Asian Elephant and the tiger preditor in the Periyar Tiger Reserve.

Day 05 – In Periyar

Early morning boat ride on Lake Periyar to watch wildlife. Later, a guided visit to a spice plantation (pepper, cardamom, cinnamon, and ginger).

Day 06 – Drive Periyar – Madurai (160 kms / 03 hrs)

Drive to Madurai, the second largest city in Tamil Nadu, located on the banks of river Vaigai. Famous for the Meenakshi Temple, a baroque example of Dravidian Architecture.

Day 07 – Drive Madurai – Trichy (140 kms / 03 hrs)

Guided tour of Madurai (Meenakshi-Sundareswarar Temple, Tirumalai Nayakar Mahal) followed by a drive to Trichy.

Day 08 – Drive Trichy – Pondicherry (255 kms / 05 hrs)

Guided tour of Trichy (Rock Fort Temple, Ranganathswamy Temple) then drive to Pondicherry, a beautiful seaside town with traces of French culture and the Sri Aurobindo Ashram.

Day 09 – Drive Pondicherry – Mahabalipuram (95 kms / 02 hrs)

Visit Sri Aurobindo Ashram and a handmade paper factory. Drive to Mahabalipuram, known for its rock-hewn temples and the world's largest stone bas-relief.

Day 10 – Mahabalipuram – Kanchipuram – Mahabalipuram

Explore the monolithic chariots and Shore Temples of Mahabalipuram. Visit Kanchipuram, known as the "City of a Thousand Temples".

Day 11 – Drive Mahabalipuram – Chennai (60 kms / 01 hr)

Drive to Chennai (Madras). Afternoon tour includes Fort St. George (built 1640), Marina Beach, and Santhome Cathedral Basilica.

Day 12 – Depart Chennai

Morning at leisure. Transfer to Chennai airport for your flight home.

Tour Package 3: Colorful Rajasthan

15 Nights / 16 Days - The ultimate Desert Journey.

A 16 day journey to the land of Princes and Rajputs, covering the best of the Desert state of Rajasthan. Stay in India's best known heritage hotels and visit iconic desert cities.

Day 01-02 – Delhi

Arrival and comprehensive tour of Old & New Delhi, including Red Fort, Jama Masjid, Chandni Chowk, and Qutub Minar.

Day 03 – Drive Delhi - Agra (220 kms/04 hrs)

Drive to Agra and visit the world-famous Taj Mahal and the massive Agra Fort.

Day 04 – Drive Agra - Samode (250 kms/05 hrs)

Drive to Samode via Fatehpur Sikri. Check into the Samode Palace, a magnificent example of Rajput-Mughal architecture with stunning frescoes.

Day 05 – Drive Samode – Mandawa (130 kms/03 hrs)

Drive to Mandawa in the heart of the Shekhawati region, known as the 'open-air art gallery'. Stay at Mandawa Castle.

Day 06 - Drive Mandawa - Bikaner (190 kms / 04 hrs)

Drive to Bikaner. Visit Junagarh Fort and the Govt-run Camel Breeding Farm.

Day 07-08 – Jaisalmer

Drive to Jaisalmer, the "Golden City". Visit Jaisalmer Fort, Jain Temples, and Patwon ki Haveli. Evening camel ride at Sam Sand Dunes.

Day 09-10 – Jodhpur

Drive to Jodhpur, the "Blue City". Visit the impressive Mehrangarh Fort and Jaswant Thada.

Day 11-12 – Udaipur

Drive to Udaipur, the "City of Sunrise". Visit City Palace, Jagdish Temple, and enjoy an evening boat cruise on Lake Pichola.

Day 13-14 – Jaipur

Drive to Jaipur, the "Pink City". Detailed tours of Hawa Mahal, City Palace, and Amber Fort.

Day 15 – Drive Jaipur – Neemrana (145 kms/03 hrs)

Drive to Neemrana Fort Palace, one of India’s oldest and finest heritage hotels, built over 10 levels on a hill.

Day 16 – Drive Neemrana – Delhi & Depart

Drive to Delhi's International Airport for your flight home.

Kashmir’s Hidden Jewels: Tarsar & Marsar

6 Nights / 7 Days of alpine trekking in Kashmir.

Known as the "prettiest trek in India," Tarsar Marsar takes you deep into the heart of the Kashmir valley. This trek is famous for its twin turquoise lakes, Tarsar and Marsar, surrounded by jagged peaks and emerald meadows. It’s a journey through nomadic shepherd paths and pristine wilderness.

Day 01 – Arrival in Srinagar & Drive to Aru

Our team will pick you up from Srinagar Airport. Enjoy a scenic 3-hour drive to Aru Valley, a quaint village near Pahalgam. Overnight in a mountain guesthouse or riverside camp.

Day 02 – Aru to Lidderwat

The trek begins! Walk through pine forests and along the Lidder River to reach the vast meadows of Lidderwat. This is a gentle and beautiful start to your adventure.

Day 03 – Lidderwat to Shekwas

Ascend into the higher alpine region. Today’s trail passes through nomadic Gujjar settlements and opens into the massive Shekwas meadows, offering panoramic mountain views.

Day 04 – Shekwas to Tarsar Lake

A short trek brings you to the crown jewel: Tarsar Lake. Camp by the shores of this turquoise alpine lake. Witness the lake change colors from turquoise to royal blue as the sun moves.

Day 05 – Tarsar to Sundarsar

Cross the Tarsar Pass and descend into the Sundarsar valley. This part of the trek is incredibly photogenic, with wildflowers blooming all around the smaller Sundarsar lake.

Day 06 – Sundarsar to Marsar & Descend to Homwas

Trek to a ridge that overlooks the elusive Marsar Lake, often hidden in a shroud of clouds. After soaking in the views, begin your descent towards the camp at Homwas.

Day 07 – Homwas to Aru & Drive to Srinagar

The final descent back to Aru Valley. Bid farewell to the mountains and drive back to Srinagar for your flight home or an evening on a traditional Houseboat.

The Alpine Wonderland: Kashmir Great Lakes

7 Nights / 8 Days covering seven legendary alpine lakes.

The Kashmir Great Lakes (KGL) trek is widely regarded as India's most beautiful trek. Over 8 days, you will cross high-altitude passes and witness seven unique alpine lakes, each reflecting the snow-capped Himalayan peaks in their crystal-clear waters.

Day 01 – Srinagar to Sonamarg

Drive from Srinagar to Sonamarg (The Meadow of Gold). Spend the evening exploring the Sindh riverbanks and preparing for the trek ahead.

Day 02 – Sonamarg to Nichnai

The trek starts with a climb through dense forests of maple and birch. Reach the Nichnai campsite, a high-altitude meadow nestled between towering peaks.

Day 03 – Nichnai to Vishansar Lake

Cross the Nichnai Pass (13,500 ft). As you descend, the magnificent Vishansar Lake comes into view—a vast sheet of blue surrounded by jagged mountains.

Day 04 – Vishansar to Gadsar via Kishansar

A challenging climb to Gadsar Pass. From the top, you can see both Vishansar and Kishansar lakes together. Descend into the flower-filled Gadsar valley.

Day 05 – Gadsar to Satsar

Trek through a rugged landscape of boulders and streams to reach Satsar—a collection of seven small interconnected lakes in a high-altitude plateau.

Day 06 – Satsar to Gangabal Twin Lakes

Cross the final pass of the trek. You'll be rewarded with a view of the twin lakes Gangabal and Nandkol, sitting at the base of the mighty Mt. Harmukh.

Day 07 – Rest Day at Gangabal

A well-deserved rest day. Explore the shores of Gangabal, try trout fishing, or simply meditate by the reflections of Mt. Harmukh.

Day 08 – Gangabal to Naranag & Drive to Srinagar

A long descent through pine forests to the ancient stone ruins of Naranag. From here, we drive you back to Srinagar, concluding a legendary journey.

Symphony of the Valleys: Exotic Kashmir

6 Nights / 7 Days exploring the crown of India.

This "Exotic Kashmir" itinerary is designed for those who want to experience the legendary beauty of the valley in comfort. From living in a luxurious hand-carved houseboat on Dal Lake to staying in the "Meadow of Flowers" (Gulmarg), this journey is a perfect blend of relaxation and sightseeing.

Day 01 – Arrival in Srinagar | Luxury Houseboat Stay

Welcome to Srinagar! Our representative will meet you at the airport and transfer you to a **Luxury Houseboat** on Dal Lake. Enjoy a relaxing 1-hour **Shikara Ride** at sunset, witnessing the floating markets and the lifestyle of the locals.

Day 02 – Srinagar to Pahalgam (The Valley of Shepherds)

After breakfast, drive to **Pahalgam**. En route, visit the saffron fields of Pampore and the ancient Awantipora ruins. Check into your hotel and enjoy the evening by the Lidder River.

Day 03 – Pahalgam Exploration (Aru & Betaab Valleys)

Spend the day exploring the breathtaking **Aru Valley**, Betaab Valley, and Chandanwari. These spots are world-famous for their lush greenery and have been the backdrop for many Bollywood films.

Day 04 – Pahalgam to Gulmarg (The Meadow of Flowers)

Drive to **Gulmarg**, one of India's premier hill stations. Gulmarg is home to one of the highest golf courses in the world. Spend the evening walking through the vast meadows filled with seasonal flowers.

Day 05 – Gulmarg (Gondola Ride & Alpine Views)

Experience the famous **Gulmarg Gondola**, one of the highest cable cars in the world. Take the ride to Phase 1 (Kungdoor) and Phase 2 (Apharwat Peak) for stunning views of the Nanga Parbat and the Pir Panjal range.

Day 06 – Gulmarg to Srinagar (Mughal Gardens)

Drive back to Srinagar. Visit the famous **Mughal Gardens**: Shalimar Bagh, Nishat Bagh, and Chashme Shahi. These 17th-century gardens are a testament to Mughal architectural elegance.

Day 07 – Srinagar Departure

Transfer to Srinagar Airport for your flight home, carrying memories of the "Paradise on Earth."
